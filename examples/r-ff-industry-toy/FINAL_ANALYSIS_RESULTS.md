# Final Analysis Results

## Project

This toy project uses public Ken French monthly data to estimate benchmark CAPM regressions for 10 industry portfolios.

- Data source: Ken French Data Library
- Sample: 2010-01 to 2024-12
- Unit of observation: industry-month
- Final sample size: 1,800 observations
- Industries: 10
- Script: `R/04_main_results.R`
- Log: `logs/04_main_results.log`

## Model

For each industry portfolio, the analysis estimates:

```text
excess_return_it = alpha_i + beta_i * market_excess_return_t + error_it
```

The estimate is descriptive benchmark evidence about market exposure across industries.

## Findings

The highest market exposure appears in Durable Goods, with a CAPM beta of 1.667.
The lowest market exposure appears in Utilities, with a CAPM beta of 0.502.
HiTec has a beta of 1.082 and a monthly alpha of 0.0028.
The market factor explains a large share of return variation for Manufacturing, HiTec, Shops, and Other, with R-squared above 0.80.

Using monthly Ken French industry portfolio data from 2010 to 2024, the final panel contains 1,800 industry-month observations across 10 industries. The HiTec portfolio has a beta of 1.082 and a monthly alpha of 0.0028. These estimates support the descriptive claim that industry portfolios differ meaningfully in market exposure over the 2010-2024 period.
