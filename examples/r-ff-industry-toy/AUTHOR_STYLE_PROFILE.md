# Author Style Profile

## Source Samples
| Sample | Path | Genre | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Toy result note | `paper/toy_results.md` | short empirical result note | Concise table-first prose |

## Discipline Baseline

Finance empirical writing. Prefer precise sample, model, coefficient, and claim-ceiling language.

## Style Dimensions
| Dimension | Observed Pattern | Apply In Manuscript | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sentence length | Short to medium | Use compact empirical claims | long setup prose |
| Hedging | Direct but bounded | State descriptive results plainly | causal verbs |
| Result narration | Table-first | Name sample, estimate, and interpretation | broad contribution claims |
| Economic magnitude | Numeric and concrete | Report beta and alpha values with context | unsupported economic welfare language |

## Reusable Sentence Moves

- "Using [data] from [period], the final sample contains [N] [unit] observations."
- "[Group] has the highest estimated [measure] at [value]."
- "These estimates support the descriptive claim that [bounded interpretation]."

## Result-Narration Preferences

Put the sample and model first, then the headline coefficient. End with the allowed claim ceiling.

## Citation Style Preferences

For this toy project, cite the data source and generated output files rather than a formal literature claim.

## Guardrails

- Journal and discipline norms override personal style.
- Do not use causal wording for the CAPM benchmark.
- Keep numeric claims traceable to `tables/table_capm_by_industry.csv`.
