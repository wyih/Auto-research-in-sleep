# Business Literature Review

## Topic

Industry-level differences in market exposure and abnormal returns using public Fama-French portfolio data.

## Conversation Map

| Stream | What It Contributes | How This Toy Uses It |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------|
| CAPM | Market beta as benchmark exposure | Estimate industry-specific market betas |
| Fama-French factors | Public factor and portfolio data for asset pricing tests | Download monthly factors and 10 industry portfolios |
| Industry portfolios | Sector-level return variation | Compare betas across NoDur, Durbl, Manuf, Enrgy, HiTec, Telcm, Shops, Hlth, Utils, Other |

## Closest Papers And Data Sources

- Sharpe-style CAPM benchmark: market excess return as the single risk factor.
- Fama-French Data Library: public monthly factor and industry portfolio data.
- Industry portfolio return studies: industry grouping as a transparent toy setting for market exposure comparisons.

## Gap For A Toy Project

This is a replication-style teaching example rather than a novel paper. Its value is workflow testing: public data download, reproducible R analysis, table/figure output, number audit, and claim discipline.

## Journal Positioning

This toy project is closest to a methods appendix or classroom replication. It is not positioned as a submission-ready accounting or finance paper.

## Takeaway

Use the project to test the business research skill chain, not to make a novelty claim.
